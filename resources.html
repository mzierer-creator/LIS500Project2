Intersectionality

Intersectionality is the combination of various traits such as race, age, class, and gender that make up an individual and the bias a person receives based on these traits. The heart of intersectionality is that each individual has a unique experience because everyone has a unique set of traits and circumstances that cumulatively define them. This term was popularized by Kimberlé Crenshaw, a critical race theory scholar and activist. The following is a lecture explaining intersectionality.

Implicit Bias

Implicit bias is unconscious prejudice held by an individual in regards to a specific group which may influence attitudes and actions towards that group. These biases can often be shaped from learned associations dictated by social norms. Everyone holds implicit biases that can relate to gender, race, age, and other traits.
